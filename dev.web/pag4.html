    Calvin Cordozar Broadus, Jr. (Long Beach, October 20, 1971), known by the stage names Snoop Doggy Dogg, Snoop Dogg, Snoop Lion and Snoopzilla is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, record producer and actor. In his entire career, Snoop has sold more than 60 million records around the world, having his career started in 1990, when he was discovered by rapper Dr. Dre, and collaborated with him on the single "Deep Cover", and on nearly every track on Dre's debut album, The Chronic.
