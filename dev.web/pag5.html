    The Strokes is an American rock band formed in 1998 in New York City. The band is formed by the members: Julian Casablancas (vocals), Albert Hammond Jr (guitar), Nick Valensi (guitar), Nikolai Fraiture (bass) and Fabrizio Moretti (drums and percussion). Bassist Nikolai Fraiture and vocalist Julian Casablancas (son of businessman John Casablancas) have been friends since childhood. Guitarist Nick Valensi and drummer Fabrizio Moretti started playing together when they were both studying at the Dwight School in Manhattan. Later, Casablancas was sent to the Le Rosey Institute, a traditional school in Switzerland, to improve his behavior; he had developed alcohol problems. There, he met Albert Hammond Jr., both Americans, despite not being very good friends. Years later, Casablancas accidentally ran into Hammond Jr. on the streets of New York.
